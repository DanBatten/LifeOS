## Race Day Pacing Strategy

### Negative Split (Recommended)
- **First Half**: 30-60 seconds slower than goal pace
- **Miles 13-20**: Settle into goal pace
- **Miles 20-26.2**: Push if feeling good

### Even Split (Alternative)
- Maintain goal pace throughout
- Requires excellent pacing discipline
- Higher risk if conditions change

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
1. Starting too fast (adrenaline)
2. Surging on hills
3. Ignoring early warning signs
4. Chasing other runners
5. Not adjusting for conditions (heat, wind)

## Heart Rate Pacing

For HR-based pacing:
- **Easy runs**: 65-75% max HR
- **Long runs**: 70-80% max HR
- **Tempo**: 80-88% max HR
- **Intervals**: 88-95% max HR
- **Race (marathon)**: 78-85% max HR

### HR Drift Considerations
- Normal: 5-10 bpm drift over long efforts
- Concerning: >15 bpm drift = dehydration or overheating
- Adjust pace to keep HR in zone, not vice versa

### Altitude Adjustment
- 3,000-5,000 ft: 3-6% slower
- 5,000-8,000 ft: 6-12% slower
- Acclimate 1-2 weeks if possible

### Wind Adjustment
- Headwind: Effort-based, not pace-based
- Expect slower splits into wind
- Make up time with tailwind (cautiously)
